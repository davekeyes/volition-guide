Guide to Volition

For organisation support staff

1. Introduction

Volition is a digital preference app and tool for Supported Decision Making.

For Decision Makers

Decision Makers — people with care and support needs — use Volition to record their preferences: what they like, what they need, and how they want to be supported.

It gives them a way to record and share their preferences on their own terms.

For support staff

As a staff supporter, Volition gives you quick, reliable access to what matters to the people you support — before, during, and after every interaction.

You have access to a Decision Maker's preference information before you walk in the door. You arrive prepared, and the person does not have to repeat themselves.

This is about better, more person-directed support.

Your role is vital.

Pilot programme — Your organisation has partnered with Volition to run a pilot programme. This guide is for staff who will use Volition as part of their work.

3

Supported Decision Making

Supported Decision Making is an approach that keeps the person at the centre of their own life. It means supporting someone to make their own decisions — not making decisions for them.

These decisions should be grounded in what is important to the person. This is sometimes called their "will and preference."

Your role as a staff supporter

Your role is to acknowledge, interpret, and respond to the person's expression of will and preference. Preferences may be communicated with words or behaviours.

This will often involve helping the person understand their options, the consequences, and act on their choices. Their voice comes first.

SDM is a human right

Recognised under the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD), which New Zealand has ratified.

The UNCRPD says that:

  • Everyone has a right to make their own decisions
  • Everyone has a right to access support to make their own decisions
  • Everyone has a right to experience the outcomes of their choices

2. Getting started

Your organisation's Account Owner will email you an invitation to join Volition.

It is important to join using this invitation, so you are connected with your organisation.

  1. 1
    Accept your invitation

    Open the invitation email and click the link to go to your Volition registration page.

  2. 2
    Register your account

    Enter your details, use your work email address, and agree to the terms of use.

You do not need a password. Volition uses email login links and optional passkeys to stay secure.

Use the Volition phone app, or go to volition.tools on any web browser, using your work email.

You will remain logged in on that device for a year, unless you log yourself out sooner.

  1. 3
    Complete your profile

    From the menu, navigate to your profile. Add your name and a photo. Decision Makers will see your profile when you are added to their Circle of Support. A clear photo and full name help build trust.

  1. 4
    Review your circle of support

    In Volition you will see the Decision Makers you have been assigned to support. By default you will have General View access.

  2. 5
    Complete your check-in

    From the Menu, tap 'Supporter check-in' and answer three questions about your experience as a supporter. Complete this at the start of your time with Volition to establish a baseline.

Before visiting a person you support:

  • Check your permission level in their profile
  • Start a Support Session before you arrive
  • Review the preferences of the person
  • Note things you want to explore or ask about

3. Using the basics

Here is what you see when you log in.

Your Circle of Support

The Decision Makers in your caseload. Click on a person's name to see their profile and explore their Volition.

Menu

Your Personal Details, activity log, passkey setup, feedback, and Supporter check-in.

8

The Vault

The Vault is the provider portal you see when you log into Volition. Hint: it has a purple background.

It is a secure web-based tool your organisation uses to manage staff access and view Decision Maker profiles. This is where you will see and interact with the preferences of the people you support.

The Vault can be accessed by downloading the Volition app, or by logging into volition.tools on any internet-connected device.

9

The Volition app

The app is what Decision Makers see when they log into Volition. Hint: it has a tan background.

From time to time you may need to navigate the app alongside the person you support — for example, to help them find a preference or update information.

Both the Vault and the Volition app are two sides of the same tool. The difference is the perspective: the Vault is yours, the app is theirs.

4. Support sessions

12

What is a support session?

A Support Session is a recorded digital interaction in Volition. When you start one, it logs the date and time, and connects your activity to the Decision Maker's record.

Starting a session is important for the audit trail. It shows the Decision Maker exactly when and how you accessed their information.

Before your next visit, open the Vault and start a Support Session to spend a few minutes reading through the person's preferences. Arrive prepared. During or after your visit, check if they would like your support to add any more information into their preference library.

13

During a session

Before you arrive

Open the Vault. Start a Support Session. Read their preferences. Note what is important, what has changed, what you want to explore together.

In the moment

Let the person lead. Reference their preferences naturally in conversation. Listen for new information or updates they would like to make.

After the visit

Close the session. Update any preferences if they asked you to. If something important came up that is not yet recorded, add it — with their knowledge.

The more consistently you do this, the richer the preference library becomes. That library is their voice.

14

How to start a session

  1. 1
    Open the Vault and find the Decision Maker's profile
  2. 2
    Tap "Explore their Volition"

    Tap "Options" if needed, then tap "Explore their Volition."

  3. 3
    Confirm the session has started

    A purple screen confirms the support session has started.

  4. 4
    Explore their preferences

    Browse and view the preferences they have shared with you.

  5. 5
    Close the session when you're done

    Tap × in the top right to end the session.

Viewing preferences

Tap "Library" to see all preferences shared with you, organised into topics — "Communication", "About Me", "Health", and more. You can browse by topic or search. Some preferences include photos, recordings, or videos.

5. Respecting preferences

10

Permissions — what you can see

Permission levels control what you can see and do. They are set by the Decision Maker — not your organisation.

There are two separate dimensions: what you see, and what you do. They each have levels, and they combine — for example, General View + Editor, or All Records + Contributor.

Visibility — what you see
General records default

You can see the Decision Maker's general preferences. You cannot see preferences they have marked as private.

All records (elevated)

You can see all preferences, including private ones. The Decision Maker has specifically trusted you with this. Private means private — respect it.

11

Permissions — what you can do

Role — what you can do
Viewer default

You can only see records.

Contributor (elevated)

You can add notes and media to existing preferences.

Editor (elevated)

You can create and edit preferences on behalf of the Decision Maker, with their agreement.

Requesting a permission change

If you believe you need a higher permission level, speak with your Manager. Do not ask the Decision Maker directly — this should go through your organisation's processes. The Decision Maker always has the final say.

15

Contributing notes and media

Contributors (elevated permission required)

As a Contributor you can add your own insights and observations.

Once in the Library, tap the preference you want to contribute to. You can:

  • Add a note
  • Upload photos, videos, audio, and files
  • Search for photos to upload online
16

Editing preferences

Editors (elevated permission required)

As an Editor you can also create, update, and modify preference records on behalf of a Decision Maker.

Creating a new preference

Tap "Create a Preference" from the home screen or library. Work through the steps together. Let them lead — your role is to help, not to decide.

Updating a preference

Tap the preference, tap "Edit," make the change together, then tap "Save." Do not edit without their direction.

Made a mistake? Tell the Decision Maker. Work with them to correct it. Don't try to quietly revert without their knowledge. If you're unsure what to do, speak to your Manager.

17

Sharing information outside the Circle

Sometimes it may be helpful to share a preference with someone outside the Circle of Support — a health professional, a travel agent, a friend, or anyone else.

  1. 1
    Open the preference or group of preferences to share
  2. 2
    Tap "Share this preference"
  3. 3
    Choose "Link" or "QR code"

    The recipient can view the preference without a Volition account. Access expires after 7 days.

6. Your responsibilities

18

Privacy and your responsibilities

When you access someone's preferences through Volition, you are handling personal information. This comes with responsibilities.

  • Only access information you need to do your job
  • Do not share someone's preferences outside of Volition without their permission
  • Do not copy or store preference information in personal devices or unapproved systems
  • If you notice incorrect information, tell your manager — do not edit without discussing it with the Decision Maker

Volition complies with the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020. For data security concerns, contact your Account Owner or info@volition.org.nz.

19

The Activity Log

The Activity Log is a record of every action taken in a Decision Maker's account — who viewed it, who added something, who edited something, and when.

As a staff member, your actions in the Vault and app are recorded in the Activity Log. This is visible to the Decision Maker. This transparency is intentional.

Decision Makers can see your activity. They can see your name in their Activity Log whenever you view or interact with their profile or preferences. They can also see your profile — your name and photo — in their Circle of Support.

If you would not want the person to know you accessed their information, ask yourself whether you should be accessing it.

Frequently asked questions

How do I log in?
Download the Volition app or go to volition.tools. Log in with your work email address. There is no password.
What if I can't see someone's preferences?
Most likely because you haven't been connected to their Circle of Support yet, or the preferences are marked private. Check with your Manager.
Can I edit a Decision Maker's preferences?
Only if you have Contributor or Editor access and the Decision Maker wants you to. Always work with the person, not for them.
A Decision Maker does not want to use Volition. What should I do?
Respect their choice. Let your Manager know. You can still support the person well using your usual methods.
Are my actions in the Vault visible to the Decision Maker?
Yes. The Activity Log shows them when you view or interact with their profile. Act accordingly.
What happens when a Decision Maker leaves our organisation?
Their preferences remain in their Volition account — their data belongs to them. Your organisation's access will be removed. Contact your Account Owner.
